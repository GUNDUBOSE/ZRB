Why Red Clay Brick

The red clay bricks have been considered to be one of the most ancient inventions ever made, which predominantly used clay as a primary raw material to build a brick. The clay bricks were often considered to be a genuine brick as most of the ancient structures ever built have become the testimony in the current context.

On the contrary, the red clay bricks have been used for all the traditional type of buildings and structures which are now evident and found around the world.

The Red Clay Brick Structure

Structurally, these red clay bricks are hand-made ones and made exclusively with clay. The clay was made to get moulded in a mould and then subjected them to fire blasting in kilns in order for the clay bricks to retain the hard structure in them, which helps in bringing the tensile strength to the structures hence constructed.

Benifits for customers from the Red Clay brick

Red clay brick has been getting used for most of the building materials such as external/internal walls, flooring, arches in both residential and commercial buildings.

  • As compared to most sidings such as timber, composites, steel, the brick never rot, never fades, never peels, melts, warps or dents past the construction.
  • As a matter of fact, the brick’s thermal mass makes it more ideal to become an insulator than compared to other sidings.
  • Last but not the least, the bricks reduces the cost of heating and cooling in the wall sidings.

Attributes in the Red Clay brick which stand unique

Over the years of red clay brick manufacturing, the bricks hence manufactured and distributed come with unmatched quality to the customer. The bricks are known to possess the following attributes:

  • Comprehensive waterproofing during the construction.
  • Ability to withstand high temperature & also the moisture to a great extent
  • The bricks have the ability to absorb less water and instead provide more strength
  • Better endurance against rain and subsequently the sunshine
  • Bricks when subjected to plastering, holds the building structure for a lifetime
